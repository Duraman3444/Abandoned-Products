{% if course.name == "Algebra I" or course.course_code == "MATH-ALG1" %}
Course Overview

Algebra I is a foundational mathematics course that introduces students to algebraic thinking and problem-solving. Students will develop skills in working with variables, equations, and mathematical relationships.

Unit 1: Foundations of Algebra
  • Real number system and properties
  • Order of operations and algebraic expressions
  • Simplifying and evaluating expressions
  • Introduction to variables and constants
Unit 2: Linear Equations
  • Solving one-step and multi-step equations
  • Equations with variables on both sides
  • Literal equations and formulas
  • Applications and word problems
Unit 3: Inequalities
  • Solving and graphing linear inequalities
  • Compound inequalities
  • Absolute value equations and inequalities
Unit 4: Linear Functions
  • Introduction to functions and relations
  • Linear functions and slope
  • Graphing linear equations
  • Writing equations from graphs and points
Unit 5: Systems of Equations
  • Solving systems by graphing
  • Substitution and elimination methods
  • Applications of systems
Unit 6: Polynomials and Factoring
  • Adding and subtracting polynomials
  • Multiplying polynomials and monomials
  • Factoring techniques
  • Solving quadratic equations by factoring
Assessment Methods
Tests & Quizzes (40%)

Unit tests, chapter quizzes, and cumulative assessments

Homework & Practice (30%)

Daily assignments, online practice, and problem sets

Projects & Participation (30%)

Group projects, presentations, and class participation

{% elif course.name == "Geometry" or course.course_code == "GEO" or "Geometry" in course.name %}
Course Overview

Geometry explores the properties of shapes, spatial relationships, and logical reasoning. Students will develop visual thinking skills and learn to construct formal mathematical proofs.

Unit 1: Foundations of Geometry
  • Points, lines, and planes
  • Segments, rays, and angles
  • Geometric notation and measurement
  • Introduction to proofs
Unit 2: Reasoning and Proof
  • Conditional statements and logic
  • Algebraic and geometric proofs
  • Properties of equality and congruence
  • Two-column and paragraph proofs
Unit 3: Parallel and Perpendicular Lines
  • Angle relationships with parallel lines
  • Proving lines parallel
  • Perpendicular lines and planes
  • Distance and slope formulas
Unit 4: Congruent Triangles
  • Triangle congruence postulates (SSS, SAS, ASA)
  • CPCTC (Corresponding Parts)
  • Isosceles and equilateral triangles
  • Triangle inequality theorem
Unit 5: Relationships in Triangles
  • Perpendicular and angle bisectors
  • Medians and altitudes
  • Inequalities in triangles
  • Indirect proof and contradiction
Unit 6: Quadrilaterals and Polygons
  • Properties of parallelograms
  • Rectangles, rhombuses, and squares
  • Trapezoids and kites
  • Interior and exterior angles of polygons

{% elif course.name == "Calculus" or course.course_code == "CALC" or "Calculus" in course.name %}
Course Overview

Calculus is the study of change and motion through derivatives and integrals. Students will explore limits, continuity, differentiation, and integration with applications to real-world problems.

Unit 1: Limits and Continuity
  • Understanding limits graphically and numerically
  • Limit laws and algebraic manipulation
  • Continuity and intermediate value theorem
  • Limits involving infinity
Unit 2: Derivatives
  • Definition of derivative
  • Power, product, quotient, and chain rules
  • Derivatives of transcendental functions
  • Implicit differentiation
Unit 3: Applications of Derivatives
  • Related rates problems
  • Linear approximation and differentials
  • Optimization problems
  • Mean value theorem
Unit 4: Integrals
  • Antiderivatives and indefinite integrals
  • Riemann sums and definite integrals
  • Fundamental theorem of calculus
  • Integration techniques
Unit 5: Applications of Integrals
  • Area between curves
  • Volume of solids of revolution
  • Arc length and surface area
  • Physics applications (work, fluid force)
Unit 6: Advanced Integration
  • Integration by parts
  • Trigonometric integrals
  • Partial fractions
  • Improper integrals

Quizzes (25%)

Homework (20%)
